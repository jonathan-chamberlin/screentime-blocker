Frequently Asked Questions

Click the Brainrot Blocker icon in your toolbar to open the popup, then click Lock In. Your work timer starts immediately and blocked sites become inaccessible.
The timer counts time you spend on productive sites or using productive desktop apps. Time spent on blocked or unrelated sites doesn't count. Once you hit your work goal (default: 50 minutes), you earn a break.
Blocked sites are sites you can't visit during a work session. Open Settings (⚙ icon) → Blocked Sites and add any domain, one per line (e.g. youtube.com). You can also click No, block this site on the productivity check popup to add a site instantly.
Confetti fires and you earn break time (default: 10 minutes). During your break, blocked sites become accessible. When break time runs out, they're blocked again automatically.
After you've been on a site for a set amount of time during a session, the extension checks in to make sure you're actually working. Click Yes, I'm working to approve the site permanently (it won't ask again). Click No, block this site to add it to your blocked list.
In Strict Mode, you cannot end your session early or adjust settings while a session is active. It's designed for people who need hard accountability with no escape hatches.
If you end a session without hitting your work goal, you can be prompted to donate to a charity (or pay someone) as a consequence. Configure the charity, amount, and payment method in Settings → Penalty Configuration.
